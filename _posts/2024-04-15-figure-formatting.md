How to use color in scientific illustration?

**Nature Figure formatting**

- single column 89 mm (3.5 inch)
- double column 183mm (7.2 inch)
- full depth 247mm (9.7 inch)
- 1.5 column 120-136mm (4.7-5.35 inch)


- **Preferred format:** tiff (300-600 dpi) for raster image; EPS or PDF for vector image

- Line weights and strokes should be set between 0.25 and 1 pt at the final size (lines thinner than 0.25 pt may vanish in print)

[Source from nature.com](https://www.nature.com/nature/for-authors/final-submission)

**Physics Review Letter**

- single column 86 mm (3.4 inch)
- double column 172 mm (6.8 inch)
- 1.5 column 129 mm (5.1 inch)
- **Preferred format:**  .ps, .eps, .pdf, .jpg, and .png
- Height of the smallest capital letters and numerals >= 2 mm; the diameter of each data point >= 1 mm; A curve’s linewidth >= 0.18 mm (0.5 pt).

[Source from APS](https://journals.aps.org/prl/authors#:~:text=Figures%20should%20have%20a%20width,all%20figures%20electronically%20for%20production.)

**Customized style**

- linewidth = 0.5 pt
- figure label: Use a 5–7 pt san serif font for standard text labelling and Symbol font for Greek characters. (Nat. Mater.)
